This page demonstrates the scenarios presented in the open-access scientific paper published in 2023, which explored what happens when the neutering prevalence of owned cats changes, and/or when the age of neutering changes. Altering either parameter can have profound effects on the overall dynamics of all cat subpopulations. Read the full paper here.

Here you can recreate the modelled scenarios explored in the published paper. The model has a starting population of 100,000 cats, runs for 10 years maximum, and assumes average model dynamics, therefore does not consider uncertainty within the parameters.
