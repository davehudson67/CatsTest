To date the cat population model has assumed a starting population structure that approximates the average cat numbers in the UK, however localised numbers of cats and their subpopulation are likely to vary geographically. For example, we know that there are more unowned cats in deprived urban areas and where there higher human population densities.

 

Our current unowned cat estimates are predicted as part or an earlier study by Cats Protection [link https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-99298-6] and restricted to urban areas. This was based on data collected from five urban regions in the UK between 2016 and 2018. There is a need to build on these estimates to understand unowned cat numbers both through time and across a larger geographic area.

 

Our long-term aim is to incorporate data on population densities and abundance of all cat types into this mapping tool as they become available to enable localised modelling.

 

In the meantime, please feel free to explore this interactive mapping tool that contains area specific estimates of unowned cat abundance where available.
